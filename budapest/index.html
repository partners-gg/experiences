Visit the Museum of Ethnography in Budapest, one of the world's oldest ethnographic museums, now housed in a striking new building at the entrance to one of the city’s major parks. Established in 1872, it was among the first specialized museums in Europe and remains a key center in Hungary for preserving and sharing cultural heritage. The museum focuses on ethnographic collections and research, helping us understand traditions and ways of life from many different places. Danube Full-Day Bend Tour from Budapest
The Danube Bend is an excursion into Hungary's history. This is an unforgettable tour along the "Blue Danube" into. some of the most pastoral scenery in Europe. **Esztergom**: The former capital of Hungary is still the centre of the Catholic Church. Visit the largest cathedral of Hungary. Afterwards, drive across the river to Slovakia and enjoy the view over the Basilica. **Visegrád**: After the Mongol invasion, a new fortification was constructed here in the mid-13th century. The top of the hill offers a fascinating panorama on the Danube valley. **Szentendre**: This small baroque city is built on medieval ruins, located at the gate of the Danube Bend. The town is considered to be the artists' village or the painters' town. Walk around the historical center and enjoy the cobblestoned narrow streets and baroque houses.

Memento Park: Private Trabant Transfer
Memento Park is a must-see for history buffs and travelers seeking an authentic experience in Budapest. With transport via a classic East German Trabant car, and a friendly personal guide, your guided tour of Memento Park will transport you back in time to communist-era Budapest. Your restored Trabant will arrive to pick you up in the Budapest area, and you'll chug along the stone streets of this historic city. Keep in mind that for safety reasons, visitors under 12 are not allowed in the Trabant. When you arrive at Memento Park you'll enjoy a complimentary beverage, and your guide will begin the informative and interactive guided tour. Feel encouraged to ask your guide questions between sculptures, monuments, and plaques to learn the historical and cultural stories woven into Hungary's complex history. Known to locals as simply "Memorial Park", notice the intentional compilation of this outdoor museum as it transitions from a painful past to a hopeful future.
