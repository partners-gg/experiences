Caves of Nerja: Entry Ticket

📍 Caves of Nerja

Wonder awaits inside the Cave of Nerja! Your ticket to the Cave of Nerja will give you access to an audio guided tour through the different areas of the cave that highlight its incredible stalagmites and stalactites. On top of that, you'll also get to enjoy a visit the Museum of Nerja to dig deeper into the caves' story. As you explore Nerja Cave, you'll be wandering through a natural laboratory where archaeological, geological and biological research has been carried out since the cave was rediscovered in 1959. Keep your eyes peeled, inside the limestone caves you'll come across the longest and largest stalactite in the world! It is located in the Hall of Cataclysm and measures a staggering 33 metres long.

Nerja & Frigiliana: Guided Tour from Granada

📍 Caves of Nerja

Experience the wonders of the Malaga region with a knowledgeable guide! Your first stop is the postcard-worthy Andalusian town of Frigiliana, boasting cobblestone streets and awe-inspiring panoramic vistas. Wander the labyrinthine alleys, and hear tales of the region's captivating history and rich cultural heritage. Then, explore the town at your own pace, before continuing onward to Nerja. Once in Nerja, you'll have ample free time to explore this coastal haven and its magnificent surroundings. Sunbathe on pristine beaches, peruse local markets brimming with vibrant wares, tuck into a traditional meal at a charming Andalusian eatery, or simply meander. Finally, journey deep into the mesmerizing subterranean world of the Nerja Caves, guided by an enlightening audio tour. Marvel at the incredible formations of stalactites and stalagmites that have gracefully evolved over thousands of years, creating a breathtaking spectacle unlike any other.

Tropical Coast & Nerja Cave: Day Trip from Granada + Lunch

📍 Caves of Nerja

Bask in the golden rays on pristine beaches, explore mysterious caves, and stroll through emblematic coastal towns. The exploration starts at Almuñécar, an ancient town founded by the Phoenicians a thousand years before Christ. Here, the Majuelo Park invites for a leisurely promenade, while the Castle of San Miguel, perched high above the city, offers sensational views of the tropical city of Granada. Next, the lovable town of Nerja awaits. Lunchtime promises a feast of local delicacies before heading to the famed Nerja Caves. These archaeological sites, part of Spain's historic heritage, will leave you breathless as you wander among a spectacular array of stalactites and stalagmites, creating a wonder of natural beauty.
