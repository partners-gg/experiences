Lausanne to Évian Ferry

📍 Lausanne Lake Cruises

Looking for a quick, money-saving way to get from one side of Lake Geneva to the other, that's also pretty easy on the planet? Simply hop on the ferry. There are up to 15 crossings every day for this roundtrip journey from Lausanne to Évian. All boats offer comfy seats and lots of opportunities to take photos of the beautiful lake. Once you're in Évian, make a trip to the source of one of the world's most famous waters - Évian, of course!

3-Hour Roundtrip Cruise from Lausanne

📍 Lausanne Lake Cruises

On this three-hour excursion into Switzerland's alpine beauty, you'll float past the Lavaux Vineyards – a UNESCO World Heritage site – admiring the villages and terraces which hug the hillsides. Then, at a comfortable rate of knots, you'll head towards the iconic Chillon Castle, a heritage site which has sat in the shadow of the Dents du Midi for around a thousand years.

Fort de Chillon History Museum: Entry Ticket

📍 Fort de Chillon

Learn about the enigmatic tactics of national defense, brought to life through interactive quizzes, immersive games, and breathtaking 3D virtual reality. Or else, walk in the shoes of a valiant soldier, accentuated by visuals and soundscapes. The museum's design, a masterpiece by the globally acclaimed François Confino, promises to impress guests. The museum also features a delightful restaurant, exclusive event rooms, a charming shop, and a panoramic terrace. From the terrace, enjoy a drink in the view of the magnificent Chillon Castle, the tranquil lake, and the majestic mountains.

Swiss Riviera Boat Tour

📍 Lausanne Lake Cruises

Switzerland’s staggering scenery is something to experience, and Montreux brings the best the country has to offer to one shoreline. Situated in the western Alps, you’ll sail the tranquil waters of Lake Geneva and pass by everything from a centuries-old castle to a wetland nature reserve. Your ship will bring you by a number of quaint villages too, but the star of the show is the Alpine views and the trees that dominate the lake’s coast.
