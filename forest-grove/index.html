Margaret River: Lake Cave Guided Tour

📍 Lake Cave

Imagine descending a spiraling staircase into a captivating sinkhole, where towering karri trees create a sunken forest. Dive into an underworld adventure and explore Lake Cave, a pristine treasure deep beneath the earth's surface. Within the cave, a tranquil lake offers a mirror image of the delicate crystal formations that dangle from the cavernous ceiling. Watch droplets cling to straw stalactites before plunging or disrupting the serene lake. The Lake Cave's star, the mysterious 'Suspended Table,' appears to defy gravity above the crystalline lake, weighing several tonnes. As the guide manipulates lighting to highlight the cave's features, you'll see some of the most stunning works of nature you've ever seen. Tours are conducted daily in small groups to provide a personalized experience.

Australia: Mammoth Cave Self-Guided Audio Tour

📍 Mammoth Cave

Mammoth Cave is a natural time capsule, home to the ancient fossil remains of long-extinct Australian Megafauna. As the most easily accessible of all the show caves in the region, you can explore this cave system without a live guide, at your own pace. Mammoth Cave is a unique window into the past. Over 10,000 fossils have accumulated inside, many of which were found to be remains of Australian Megafauna (giant animals) that became extinct around 46,000 years ago. Plug into your audio guide and let history unravel in your ears as you explore and finally come face-to-face with a 50,000 year old ‘'zygomaturus' jawbone, embedded in the cave wall!
