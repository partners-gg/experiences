BOLOGNA ·PALAZZO FAVA
Palazzo Fava: Michelangelo and Bologna Exhibition
Bologna was the city that welcomed Michelangelo twice, first as a young man seeking refuge, and later as a renowned master. On both occasions, it shaped his artistic journey in meaningful ways. This enduring connection is the starting point of the exhibition *Michelangelo and Bologna*, hosted at Palazzo Fava from 14 November 2025 to 15 February 2026, which marks the 550th anniversary of Michelangelo Buonarroti’s birth (1475–1564). As the curators highlight, the exhibition aims to restore Bologna’s crucial role in the evolution and refinement of Michelangelo’s genius. The exhibition reconstructs the artist’s two decisive stays in Bologna. This is organized into six sections and features more than fifty works, from marble sculptures and drawings to rare books and archival materials.

BOLOGNA ·ARCHIGINNASIO OF BOLOGNA
Archiginnasio of Bologna: Audio Guide + Typical Food Tasting
Founded way-back-when in 1088 and still going strong today, the University Bologna is the world's oldest continuously operating university. It was the first institution to award degrees, and is also where we got the word *university* itself. It's a pretty big deal! The original university building was housed in the astounding Archiginnasio of Bologna, where this audio-guided tour will take you on a journey through time, after you skip the line. Located in the historic center of Bologna, it's one of the city's most important buildings and a cultural must-see. This tour focuses on the interesting history of the University of Bologna, as you explore the magnificent Municipal Library and the 16th-century Anatomical Theater, once used for anatomy lessons. Almost entirely destroyed by bombs in WWII, it was restored using all of the original rubble. At the end of the tour, you'll get a tasting platter of delicious local cold cuts in the nearby Quadrilatero market.

BOLOGNA ·SANCTUARY OF THE MADONNA DI SAN LUCA
Sanctuary of the Madonna di San Luca: Guided Tour
Join a guided tour of Basilica of San Luca with an expert to learn about the history, stories, and unique aspects of the basilica. Built between the 17th and 18th centuries, it contains the Madonna and Child icon, considered to protect the locals. This icon, thought to have arrived in the 12th century, is central to many traditions and rituals. Find the basilica on Colle della Guardia, accessible via the Portico of San Luca, which is nearly 4 km long with 666 arches. This portico has served pilgrims for centuries. Inside, see the dome and altars, while the façade offers views of the hills and city.
