Super Safari: Quad, Jeep, Camel & Party with Dinner

📍 Outdoor Activities in Port Ghalib

Desert Safari at the Red Sea: Revel in the thrill of racing a Quad Bike through the captivating desert landscape and majestic Red Sea mountains. Enjoy a two-hour adrenaline-fueled ride, weaving through the swirling sand dunes all the way to a traditional Bedouin village. There, guests can indulge in the authentic local culture by sipping on aromatic black tea and embarking on an enchanting camel ride. The adventure begins with a convenient pick-up from the hotel and a short trip to the Quad Bike station. There, participants can familiarize themselves with their vehicle, learning all the crucial details before hitting the desert trails. Feel the desert breeze on your face and the adrenaline pumping through your veins as the desert flashes by. But hold on, the fun isn't over yet! The next exhilarating chapter involves a Jeep ride that elevates the adventure to a whole new level. After an hour of high-octane action, it's time to slow down for a serene 15-minute camel ride. Finally, the grand finale at the Bedouin village beckons! Here, guests can unwind, soak in the Bedouin lifestyle, and enjoy the mesmerizing sunset over the Red Sea mountains. As the evening draws to a close, it's time to sit back and savor a freshly cooked meal under the starlit desert sky. This is a ticket to an unforgettable adventure, so book now and create memories that last a lifetime!

Marsa Mubarak: Snorkeling with Dugong and Sea Turtles

📍 Diving and Snorkeling Port Ghalib

Take a trip to Marsa Alam, home to the sea cow (Dugong) and the largest turtles in the Red Sea. In the protected bay of Marsa Mubarak, you'll find a vibrant coral reef full of life. This experience lets you get up close with some rare and fascinating sea creatures. Whether you're a family, a group of adults, or a couple looking for adventure, this trip has something for everyone. It starts with a convenient pickup from your hotel in a comfortable, air-conditioned vehicle to Port Ghalib Marine, just 1 km south of Marsa Alam Airport. From there, you'll sail for 25 minutes to the Marsa Mubarak Spot. Your guide will give a short briefing on the local marine life and safety instructions. Then, dive in and search for the star of the show – the Dugong.
