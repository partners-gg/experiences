Lascaux IV: Guided Tour

📍 Lascaux International Center of Parietal Art

Lascaux IV, also known as the International Centre for Cave Art, features a full-scale replica of the Lascaux Cave. A guided tour takes you through the replica and explains the cave’s artwork and history. After the tour, you can visit additional areas such as the Workshop, Theater, Gallery of the Imagination, and Immersion Room, which provide further context on prehistoric life and cave art. Interactive displays and digital tools help explain the techniques, meaning, and significance of the artwork, as well as its influence on later art forms.

Lascaux II: Guided Tour

📍 Lascaux International Center of Parietal Art

Lascaux II is the first replica of the original cave, which was closed to the public in 1940. It is located about 50 m from the original site. During a guided tour, you’ll visit two reproduced areas, the Hall of the Bulls and the Axial Diverticulum. The tour explains how the cave was discovered and provides background on the artwork. The visit takes place in a controlled setting designed to reflect the original cave environment.

Vézère Valley

📍 Lascaux International Center of Parietal Art

Learn about prehistoric art and culture through this carefully curated package. Go on a self-guided tour of Lascaux IV, using an audio guide to understand the cave art's significance, and visit the Laugerie-Basse site, a UNESCO-recognized archaeological site, a learning experience for all ages. Explore the Grand Roc Cave with a guided tour to see geological formations and participate in Thot Park's interactive exhibits showcasing prehistoric wildlife.
