📍 Phillip Island Nature Parks

Visit the Koala Conservation Reserve on Phillip Island to see Australian wildlife. Walk along two boardwalks to spot koalas, wallabies, echidnas, and native birds. Phillip Island Nature Parks: Penguin Parade Entry Ticket

📍 Phillip Island Nature Parks

Watch the little penguins make their way across the beach at the Penguin Parade, surrounded by local wildlife like swamp wallabies, eastern barred bandicoots, cape barren geese, and short-tailed shearwaters. Wander the boardwalks at night to see these penguins return to their burrows along with other local animals. Each ticket supports the preservation of this habitat, helping Nature Parks rangers restore and protect the landscape for the penguins and other wildlife. Choose from two viewing options: the Penguins Plus for a close-up view with limited capacity by the penguin path, or the General Viewing area for a broad look at the penguins as they waddle to the colony.

Phillip Island: Churchill Island Entry Ticket

📍 Phillip Island Nature Parks

Visit Churchill Island in Victoria, a place where history and nature come together. Walk through open spaces and historic grounds with fresh air. Explore old cottages, see horse-drawn carts, and walk through maintained gardens and lawns. View the coastline overlooking Phillip Island. Learn about Australia's Indigenous and pioneering history at the first agricultural site from the 1850s. Walk around the farm, follow bay trails, and watch traditional farming activities like sheep shearing, working dogs, and cow milking, demonstrated by the island's rangers and farmhands.

Cape Woolamai Cruise from San Remo

📍 Phillip Island Cruises

This scenic cruise gives you a whole new perspective on the pristine beaches os Phillips Island, not to mention a superior scope on the magnificent Cape Woolamai cliffs. Your guide for this one-hour cruise from San Remo will regale you with stories of these spectacular surroundings before you cruise up close to the base of the Cape Woolamai cliffs. This is a popular Phillip Island surfing spot and one of the island's most scenic treasures. You'll get tea or coffee with cake to line your stomach, not to lure the dolphins in, but they often swim by so keep your eyes peeled!

Penguin Parade + Moonlit Sanctuary Wildlife Park: Day Trip from Melbourne

📍 Phillip Island Nature Parks

Departing from Melbourne, this trip takes participants to the region's most cherished natural gems, including the Moonlit Sanctuary Wildlife Conservation Park, The Nobbies boardwalk, and the famed Penguin Parade. In the Moonlit Sanctuary, the Australian wilderness comes alive with kangaroos, wallabies, reptiles, Tasmanian devils, and Koalas. For a small extra charge, adventurers can get up close and personal with a Koala or wallaby! Next, The Nobbies offers breathtaking views of the Bass Strait from its boardwalks. Stroll along the clifftop paths and enjoy views of The Nobbies and Seal Rocks. Last but not least is the Penguin Parade on Phillip Island. There, spectators can marvel as adorable little penguins hustle back to their burrows from the sea.

📍 Phillip Island Cruises

Join in on a leisurely evening cruise through the sheltered waters of Western Port Bay on a modern catamaran. Make your way around Churchill Island, Reef Island, Elizabeth Island, and the southern side of French Island as a perfect way to end your day at the beautiful Phillip Island.
