Kerid Crater: Entry Ticket

📍 Kerid Crater

Visit Kerid Crater in Iceland's Golden Circle. This 6,500-year-old volcanic crater features red rock, green moss, and a blue lake at the bottom. Formed by a collapsed magma chamber, Kerid measures 55 m deep, 170 m wide, and 270 m across. Walk along the crater rim or take steps down to the lake.

Geothermal Exhibition

📍 Hellisheiði Geothermal Power Plant

Over 70% of Iceland's energy consumption is powered by geothermal energy - that's a higher percentage than anywhere else on the planet! Take a tour of the Geothermal Exhibition at Hellisheiði (pronounced “het-li-shay-thee”) - the newest and biggest geothermal power plant in Iceland. Enjoy a self-guided audio tour of the exhibition, which allows you to see into the turbine halls, look over the working areas from the observation decks, and explore the world of geothermal power.
