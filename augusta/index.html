Cape Leeuwin Lighthouse: Guided Tour

📍 Cape Leeuwin Lighthouse

Explore Cape Leeuwin Lighthouse, at Australia's south-westerly tip, where the Southern and Indian Oceans meet. This is mainland Australia's tallest lighthouse and quite a sight to behold. Discover the 1895 lighthouse constructed from local limestone, guiding vessels through the perilous cape. Your guided tour ascends to the tower's top, revealing a stunning seascape of frothy waves and wild shoreline. Within the confines of an original lighthouse keeper's cottage, you'll hear tales of those who served as guardians of this formidable coastline. And, if the stars align between May and September, you might catch the sight of whales on their annual pilgrimage along the coastline.

Margaret River, Mammoth Caves & Cape Leeuwin Lighthouse: Guided Tour + Lunch

📍 Cape Leeuwin Lighthouse

Travel south from Perth through farmland and coastal towns to Busselton, the gateway to the Margaret River region. Here, you can have a morning tea (at extra cost) at the Busselton Jetty, the longest timber jetty in the southern hemisphere. Next, head to a boutique brewery in Margaret River for lunch (subject to change) with free beer and cider tastings. The tour also includes a wine tasting. Visit Mammoth Cave for a guided tour of its chambers and learn about the local flora and fauna. Continue to Cape Leeuwin Lighthouse, where the Southern and Indian Oceans meet. After a stop in Margaret River township, return to Perth around 8:30 PM.
