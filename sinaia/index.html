Peleș Castle: Entry Ticket

📍 Peleș Castle

As far as European castles go, Peleș is pretty young. In fact, it was the first castle to be fully powered by electricity, and running water wasn't an afterthought. This palace tour allows you to explore the decadent residence and its numerous rooms, including a 60-seat theater and a library with a secret door. From its frescoed ceilings and stain glass roof to the 4,000 or so European and Asian artifacts collected in the armory, get an insight into royal life.

Pelișor Castle: Fast Track Ticket

📍 Pelișor Castle

Built at the turn of the 20th century, Pelişor was the last Queen of Romania's summer home, together with her husband, Ferdinand I. Queen Marie was renowned for the control she exerted over her husband, and the palace's varied interior is proof of her ability to get what she wanted. Though it's part of Peleș Castle complex, Pelişor's Art Nouveau style has a distinctiveness to it. The Queen's taste remains strong throughout, as does her heart – which can be found waiting for you inside!
