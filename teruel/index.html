When you think of Spain, dinosaurs likely aren't the first subject that comes to mind. Mausoleum of the Lovers of Teruel

📍 Mausoleum of the Amantes

In September 2005, the current Mausoleum of the Lovers was inaugurated. The project of the building, designed by the architect Alejandro Cañada, has different exhibition rooms that aim to bring the History of the Lovers closer to the visitor. The Mausoleum of the Lovers – opened in 2005 and designed by architect Alejandro Cañad – is organised around four sectors and invites you to come closer to the history of the Lovers of Teruel. Sector I: _Love in Difficult tTimes_, offers insight into the social, political and cultural characteristics that surrounded the events in Teruel at the beginning of the 13th century. Sector II: _The History of the Lovers_, tells the story of events that took place in Teruel in 1217 between Juan Martínez de Marcilla and Isabel de Segura. It also discusses the historical debate that this story has generated over the centuries.
