Dr. Sun Yat-Sen Chinese Garden: Entry Ticket + Guided Tour Option

A bit of tranquillity and reflection can do a world of good when the hum of city life gets a bit too loud. Vancouver's Dr. Sun Yat-Sen Classical Chinese Garden has been providing just that since 1986. Meander the serene paths, catching glimpses of koi fish and turtles as you go, and admire picturesque pagodas and reflections cast from the ponds. This Ming Dynasty-style garden-home is the first among its kind to have been built outside of China, and is a unique venue for cultural programming and events, including guided tours, concerts, festivals, exhibitions, receptions, educational and cultural programs. One of Vancouver's top tourist attractions, it's no wonder why it was Canada’s Garden of the Year in 2012 and named the 'World's Top City Garden' by National Geographic.

📍 Vancouver Maritime Museum

Sail into Vancouver's maritime history at the Vancouver Maritime Museum in the picture-perfect Vanier Park on the Vancouver waterfront. Learn about the profound relation-"ship" between humans and the sea, as you explore model ships, an extensive collection of maritime art, retired vessels, a huge library, and archives. Outdoors, you'll find the NASA undersea research vessel Ben Franklin, and the boiler of the Beaver, the first steamship in the Pacific Northwest. The famed St. Roch is a National Historic Site. This RCMP vessel was the first vessel to circumnavigate North America in the early 20th century and helped to connect distant communities in the Canadian Arctic. It makes you realize just how far we've come with communication!
