📍 The Bell Tower

Move aside Quasimodo – it's your turn to shout "The bells, the bells!" at Perth's famous Bell Tower, home of the Swan Bells. These bells were donated by the cities of London and Westminster. Motor Museum of Western Australia: Guided Tour

📍 Motor Museum of Western Australia

Visit the Motor Museum of Western Australia in Whiteman Park. It's 30 minutes northeast of Perth and on the way to Swan Valley. Learn about over a century of car history. Take a guided tour at 11:00 or 14:00, which lasts about 45 minutes and is included with your entry. Alternatively, walk through the exhibits on your own, which takes about one to two hours. See Daniel Ricciardo’s 2014 Red Bull RB10, the only winning Formula 1 race car on public display in Australia. The museum has over 200 cars and motorcycles from the late 1800s to today. There are also more than 15,000 scale models, the largest collection in the Southern Hemisphere. Murals by local artists also highlight local motoring history.

VERTIGO at Optus Stadium

📍 Optus Stadium

The VERTIGO experience at Optus Stadium allows sports fans to step off the roof and walk out on a platform that takes you beyond the edge of Australia's third-largest stadium. Harnessed from above and with no handrails, the more adventurous can push their senses to the limit and dare to lean and look down over the edge! _Gulp_. The western viewing deck at Optus Stadium (Perth Stadium for the traditionalists) allows people of all abilities to enjoy a new, uninterrupted view of Perth and the Derbarl Yerrigan (Swan River). This is the first fully accessible rooftop experience at an Australian stadium, providing incredible views of Perth as well as the hallowed Perth Stadium turf. The new VERTIGO structure weighs 70 tonnes and was built off-site in 15 modular sections, being craned into place during an operational stadium calendar that included three AFL finals, including the AFL Grand Final.
