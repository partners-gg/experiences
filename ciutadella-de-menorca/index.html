Cova de S’Aigua: Guided Tour

📍 Cova de S’Aigua

The Cova de s'Aigua, literally ‘cave of water’, will draw you in with its prehistoric past and secluded lake with incredibly clear water. Join a guide and go underground. They’ll take you along a route with incredible geological formations, such as stalactites and stalagmites, to admire. The star of the show, though, is the crystal-clear lake. Expect an almost otherworldly beauty as you peer down to its rugged, rocky bottom. This guided tour of the cave will reveal more about its history and the work that was done for its recovery and conditioning to be visited.

Naveta d'Es Tudons: Entry Ticket

📍 Naveta d'Es Tudons

During the Iron Age, the Talaiotic people began to build stone tombs, _navetas_, for their burial rites. The Naveta d'Es Tudons is the largest and best preserved of those. Made up of two chambers – the lower chamber used for the bones of the dead after the flesh had been removed, and the upper for the drying of recently placed corpses – the Naveta d'Es Tudons has stood the test of time and remains remarkably well preserved despite facing the elements outside and having no mortar to hold the stones in place. The Naveta d'Es Tudons is believed to have been used between 1400 and 900 BC and it offers a unique glimpse into what life was like for the island societies of Menorca and Mallorca during the Iron Age.

📍 Menorca Cruises

See the northern coast, including the Cavalleria Lighthouse, Illa d’es Porrus, Cala Viola, the ancient Roman port of Sanitja, Cala Mica, and Cala Rotja.
