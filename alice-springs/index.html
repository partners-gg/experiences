ALICE SPRINGS ·ALICE SPRINGS DESERT PARK
Alice Springs Desert Park: Entry Ticket
Explore Australia's arid wilderness at the Alice Springs Desert Park, a gateway to the outback that fascinates visitors worldwide. Located near Alice Springs and under the West MacDonnell Ranges, the park celebrates the heritage of the local Arrernte people and showcases the Akngwelye and Yeperenye Altyerre (wild dog and caterpillar) dreamtime stories. The park features three desert ecosystems, Desert Rivers, Sand Country, and Woodland, each highlighting the rugged beauty and resilient life of the desert. You can enjoy guided presentations, a free-flying bird show, and rare encounters with endangered species at the Nocturnal House. Managed by the Northern Territory Government and the Parks and Wildlife Commission, the park focuses on conservation, research, and breeding programs, earning Advanced Ecotourism Accreditation.

ALICE SPRINGS ·ALICE SPRINGS DAY TOURS
Uluru & Kata Tjuta: Guided Sunrise Tour + Transfers
Start the day early to visit two landmarks in Uluru–Kata Tjuta National Park. Begin the morning at Talinguru Nyakunytjaku, the sunrise viewing platform, where the first light reveals Uluru against the desert horizon. Next, travel 45 kilometers to Kata Tjuta. Listen to the driver guide as they share stories and historical details about the park. Upon arrival, take a guided walk through Walpa Gorge. The towering rock walls and rare plant species offer a contrast to the surrounding red desert landscape. This tour highlights the unique features of Uluru, Kata Tjuta, and Walpa Gorge in the Red Centre.
