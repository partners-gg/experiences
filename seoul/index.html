N Seoul Tower: Entry Ticket

📍 N Seoul Tower

Visit N Seoul Tower, also known as Namsan Tower, in central Seoul. Standing at 774 ft on Namsan Mountain, it has been a city symbol since 1980. View the city from its observation decks, especially at night with the city lights. You can reach the tower via hiking, cable car, or shuttle bus through Namsan Park. At the tower's base, find cultural attractions, restaurants, and shops. The "Locks of Love" fence is a popular spot where couples leave padlocks. N Seoul Tower also offers exhibitions and light shows, blending natural surroundings, cultural elements, and modern entertainment.

Seoul Sky Lotte World Tower: Entry Ticket

📍 Seoul Sky Lotte World Tower

Visit Lotte World Tower in Seoul, South Korea. Standing at 555 m tall with 123 floors, this skyscraper has something for everyone. Find offices, a luxury hotel, malls, and various dining options. Start at the observatory on the 117th floor for wide views of Seoul, including the Han River, especially clear at sunset and night. Look straight down through the glass sky deck on the 118th floor. At the top, a restaurant and café offer meals with city views. Inside, Lotte World Mall and duty-free shops provide many shopping choices.

Seoul Royal Palace Tour

📍 Gyeongbokgung Palace

Start your day with a guided visit to Gyeongbokgung Palace, the largest and most beautiful of Seoul's Five Grand Palaces. Watch the Royal Guard Changing Ceremony held at the palace. After the village tour, head to Bukchon Hanok Village to see traditional Korean houses (hanoks), many of which now serve as cultural centers, guesthouses, and restaurants. If it’s closed, the tour will go to Namsangol Hanok Village instead. Then take a drive along Bugak Skyway, passing Cheongwadae (the former presidential residence). Stop at Bugak Palgakjeong Pavilion for views of the city. Next, visit the Cheongha Korea Ginseng Center to learn about Korean ginseng, historically valued for its medicinal properties. End the tour at Gwangjang Market, where you can shop and try local street food like mung bean pancakes and seaweed rice rolls.

DMZ: Second Tunnel & Sky Bridge Full Day Guided Tour from Seoul

📍 Day Trips from Seoul

Take a historical journey through the 2nd Tunnel, a relic nestled 13 km north of Cholwon within the Korean Demilitarized Zone (DMZ), between North and South Korea. Unearthed on March 19, 1975, this tunnel could once accommodate up to 30,000 armed soldiers in just sixty minutes, complete with vehicles, artillery, and even tanks. Then, take a walk on the Sky Bridge, revealing views of the scenery below.
