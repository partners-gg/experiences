VITERBO ·VITERBO SOTTERRANEA
Viterbo Underground
The labyrinth under the city of Viterbo has seen its share of drama over the centuries. It is thought to date back to the Etruscan era, but we can say with certainty the tunnel network was used as a secret escape route during sieges or invasions in the Middle Ages. It came into use again during WWII, acting as an air raid shelter. There are untold stories lurking in the shadows. Pluck up your courage and enter the darkness on this 20-minute guided tour around Viterbo's subterranean world.

Palace of the Popes (Polo Monumental Colle del Duomo): Skip The Line

The palace still retains the partial appearance of a military fortress, which it was back in 1255, before being transformed into a papal court. Viterbo Pass
A stone's throw from Rome lies Viterbo, a medieval gem nestled in Tuscia, the land of the ancient Etruscans. It's best known for being the seat of the papacy for two decades during the 13th century, but your Viterbo Pass gets you access to lots of interesting places! Step back in time via the Palazzo dei Priori with its frescoes from the 1500s, and the Palazzo dei Papi, the site of the first and longest Conclave in history. It's all about the atmosphere at the Rossi Danielli Civic Museum, located in the cloister and the convent annexed with the church of Santa Maria della Verità. Uncover treasures at the Museum of Ceramics and the Museum of the Sodalizio dei Facchini di Santa Rosa. Or imagine the audience in awe at the Teatro dell'Unione, one of the most beautiful Italian theaters in Lazio. The choice is yours with the Viterbo Pass.
