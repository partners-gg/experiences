Mount Etna: Jeep Tour, Mount Sartorius + Thieves' Cave

📍 Mount Etna

Kick off your extraordinary trip at the foot of Mount Etna, guided by a seasoned expert who will regale you with tales of the volcano's rich history and culture. Travel through lesser-known paths, soaking in the untouched flora and fauna, far from the hustle and bustle of tourist crowds. Admire the scenic landscapes etched by relentless lava flows and the resilience of nature in the face of such potent destruction. The adventure begins in the town of Milo in Etna Park. From here, gaze at the spectacular Ionian coast with the scenic views of Taormina and the towering summit of the volcano. Venture further to the northeast side of the Etna volcano, where you'll explore the Sartorius Mountains. These mountains, a collection of seven dormant craters, bear the scars of the 1865 eruption. You'll find a forest of pristine white birch trees, a testament to the unparalleled natural beauty that Sicily proudly possesses.

Mount Etna: Excursion to the Summit Craters from Etna Sud

📍 Mount Etna

Most excursions on Mount Etna stop at the Silvestri Craters, at relatively low altitude – but with this exclusive tour, you can reach the top of the highest active volcano in Europe with a dedicated volcanologist guide. Take a cable car and 4WD to Torre del Filosofo, 2900 meters above the ground, and continue on foot for approximately 3 km until you reach the summit craters. Your expert guide will share the history of the volcano with you, so you can learn the story of Mount Etna while you admire the moon-like landscape stretching out to the coast.

Mount Etna: Summit and Crater Trek from the Northern Side

📍 Mount Etna

The climb to the Summit Craters is a unique life experience that has as its subject and location the North Side of Etna. In the town of Piano Provenzana, your guide will take a few minutes to go through a safety briefing. Then, the climb will take place with the use of special 4X4 vehicles (available for an extra fee) to lead you to an altitude of 2950 m. After taking some photos of the landscape, the trek will go towards the ancient and recent lava fields to reach the edge of the Summit Craters at an altitude of 3330 m. The climb continues across the entire Summit area, admiring the craters of the North-East, the South-East, the Bocca Nuova, and the Voragine. The trek around the summit area could change depending on the recent volcanic activity. The descent continues through volcanic sands to reach Piano delle Concazze. The breathtaking panorama will show the Valle del Leone and the most famous Valle del Bove, giving participants a unique and unforgettable view.
