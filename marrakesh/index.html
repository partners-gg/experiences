Comptoir des Mines Galerie: Guided Tour

📍 Comptoir des Mines Galerie

Built in 1932, this 1,500 m2 Art Deco building originally housed the Comptoir des Mines, a trading company for mining products. The space was designed with offices, apartments, and warehouses, spreading over two wings, two floors, and includes an attached hangar. After being abandoned in the 1990s for over two decades, it was repurposed for the 2016 Marrakesh Biennale, assuming a new function. The preserved Art Deco features, including terrazzo flooring and walls, staircases, and windows, create a unique atmosphere. Since the gallery's establishment, restoration has carefully adhered to its original style, incorporating period switches, chandeliers, and doors. Further renovations in 2023 and 2024 opened spaces to bring in light and depth.

Marrakesh: Jardin Majorelle Guided Tour + Transfers

📍 Marrakesh City Tours

Visit the Majorelle Garden. This botanical garden includes fountains, ponds, bamboo groves, cacti, and a private museum collection. The space displays over 15 species of North African birds. Cobalt-blue buildings accentuate the garden, which was designed by the French artist Louis Majorelle and later restored by Yves Saint Laurent. View the walls of Marrakesh, notable for their red stone structure and historical gates. These walls, exceeding 10 km in length, stand around 7.6 to 10.8 m high and were historically significant as urban enclosures. Ten gates provide entry, with Bab Khemis being among the prominent ones. Some gates have an architectural influence on other Moroccan cities.
