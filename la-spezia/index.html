LA SPEZIA ·CINQUE TERRE DAY TRIPS
Cinque Terre: Guided Boat Tour
Visit the Cinque Terre coast by boat and view the vineyards and buildings from the sea. Stop in Portovenere, then Monterosso and Vernazza along the way. Depart from the port of La Spezia and sail through the Gulf of Poets. Sail to Portovenere and see the beauty of the village. In Monterosso al Mare, explore the medieval historic center and the parish church dedicated to St. John the Baptist. Afterward, continue sailing towards Vernazza, the pearl of the Cinque Terre. Vernazza appears to visitors like the bow of a ship; most of the town is in fact built on a small promontory that ends at the sea with an imposing fortification system. At the end of the tour, spend some free time sampling local wines and foods for lunch, or take a swim in the crystal-clear Mediterranean water before returning to La Spezia.

LA SPEZIA ·CINQUE TERRE BOAT TOURS
Cinque Terre & Gulf of Poets: Boat Tour with Lunch & Drink
Departing from Lerici, La Spezia, or Portovenere (Monterosso for private tours), the boat will take you towards the Gulf of Poets and Portovenere. Known as the "sixth town of Cinque Terre," this historic area features the striking Byron Cave and the Church of San Pietro. Take photos here before a refreshing swim and a taste of focaccia. As you head towards Cinque Terre, discover the hidden villages between Portovenere and Riomaggiore, visible only from the sea. Swim near a natural waterfall before entering the Cinque Terre National Park. Sail past the five towns, stopping near Corniglia to explore a small cave and dive from the cliffs. Arriving in Monterosso, you will spend a brief 40 minutes enjoying ice cream and shopping for souvenirs. Then, you will return to Portovenere, Lerici, or La Spezia accompanied by good music.

LA SPEZIA ·CINQUE TERRE DAY TRIPS
Cinque Terre: Riomaggiore and Monterosso Guided Boat Tour
Depart from La Spezia and navigate around the Gulf of Poets, all while admiring the beauty of this part of Eastern Liguria. The local landscape with its famous terraced vineyards is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and you'll soon seen why! Visit the village of Riomaggiore with its beautiful marina, then walk along the maze-like streets and stairways to the church of San Giovanni Battista. Enjoy some free time for sightseeing before boarding for Monterosso, where you'll be able to visit the medieval old town, and walk along the scenic seafront to reach Torre Aurora – from here, you can get a magnificent view over Fegina Beach. Take some time to relax, unwind, and take some amazing photos, then head back to La Spezia.
