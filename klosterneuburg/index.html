Klosterneuburg Abbey: Guided Wine Cellar Tour & Tasting

📍 Stift Klosterneuburg

The wine cellar of the winery Stift Klosterneuburg lies about 36 metres underground and maintains a constant temperature between 12 and 14 degrees Celsius all year around This is due to a 300-year-old, natural ventilation system that provides a relaxed climate behind the strong walls. This atmospheric wine cellar tour will introduce you to the stunning baroque cellar ensemble, which, supported by meter-thick brick walls, extends four levels deep underground. The combination of centuries-old winemaking tradition and state-of-the-art cellar technology can be experienced in a unique way in the oldest winery in Austria. The tour highlights the 900-year history of wine production in Klosterneuburg. After the tour there is the possibility of a wine tasting in the Stiftsvinothek, a cellar vault from the 13th century.

Klosterneuburg Abbey: Grand Guided Tour in German

📍 Stift Klosterneuburg

With a licensed, German-speaking expert as your guide (accompanied by a multilingual headset), dive into 900 years of medieval history with a "grand" guided visit to the Klosterneuburg Abbey – including some areas not accessible with a general admission ticket. This sprawling complex reveals centuries of Vienna's history where your guided visit will give you fascinating insights into this spectacular structure and its renowned inhabitants. See the famous Verdun Altar, one of the most precious pieces of Middle Ages art that graces the tomb of St. Leopold. Gaze at the sacred crown of Austria, adorned with gold and precious stones – the showpiece of the Treasury Chambers. Your grand guided tour concludes in the Abbey Church, which is particularly impressive due to its baroque décor, and once served as the imperial oratory of Charles VI.
