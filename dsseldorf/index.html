Düsseldorf: Old Town Guided Walking Tour with Maritime Museum
Prepare for a tour to learn about the culture, 700-year-old history, and nightlife of Düsseldorf's old town, which covers just half a square kilometre. Discover over 260 bars, clubs, cafés, and restaurants here, which is why it’s often called “the longest bar in the world.” Explore local landmarks, from old churches and market squares to modern museums and galleries. On a guided tour, locals will share stories like the tradition of the cartwheel, the legacy of Elector Jan Wellem, or the festive awakening of Hoppeditz during Carnival. The nearby Andreas Quartier shows how old buildings can be used in new ways. The tour ends with a walk along the Rhine promenade, where you can view the river, bridges, and meadows.

DÜSSELDORF ·DÜSSELDORF CITY TOURS
Düsseldorf: Sushi, Sake & Little Tokyo Tour
In the heart of Düsseldorf, you'll find 'Little Tokyo,' Europe's third-largest Japanese community. Nowhere else in Europe showcases Japanese life so vividly in one area. Award-winning restaurants and Izakayas (traditional Japanese pubs) offer authentic Japanese cuisine, recognized as an Intangible Cultural Heritage in 2013. You will find ramen stalls, bakeries, bars, hotels, supermarkets, and bookstores along Immermannstraße and Klosterstraße, from the central station to the city center, creating a unique Japanese vibe. Locals, Japanese businesspeople, young manga fans, and foodies from around the world gather here. Over bowls of ramen, sushi, and sake, they bring life to the Japanese Quarter, making it one of Düsseldorf's most lively spots.
